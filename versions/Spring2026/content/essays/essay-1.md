---
title: How AI Has Changed My Work, Thinking, and Daily Life
author: Sanjana Shivanand
order: 1
---

When ChatGPT came out in 2022 it felt like a key moment in how people use technology. For the time artificial intelligence was something regular users could directly work with not just something in research labs or big companies. At that time, I did not really get how important it was. To me ChatGPT was a helpful tool that made schoolwork a bit easier. During my bachelor's studies my use of AI was simple and not very deep. I used ChatGPT to rephrase assignments get complex topics explained or find answers when I was stuck. It felt like it saved time but a bit, on the surface. I did not really question the answers I got or think about how ChatGPT came up with them. Artificial intelligence was another quick way to get things done like using a search engine. I did not think ChatGPT would change how I learned or solved problems.

During my internship in India AI acted as a bridge that helped my team build and learn at the time. This learn-as-you-go method was really empowering at first. It gave me the confidence to take on concepts even if I did not fully understand them. However, we soon faced problems. We followed AI suggestions without getting the logic behind them and we struggled to fix issues when they came up. It taught me that AI can speed up work, but it cannot replace a basic understanding. Moving fast is only helpful if you are on the track.

I got curious about AI beyond technical tasks and tried using it in a more personal way. I chatted with @MetaAI on WhatsApp like it was a digital diary. I shared my thoughts and feelings with it where, at first, it seemed like an idea. It was convenient to get responses, but over time it got boring. The responses were repetitive and too long. They lacked emotional depth. It felt like I was just getting patterns from a system and not a conversation.  It felt like AI is just mimic talking and not having human connection. I realised that AI can give responses that sound thoughtful, but it does not truly get emotions or experiences.

After moving to the United States, my exposure to AI increased significantly. I began to see how widely it was used—not just in classrooms, but across industries and everyday workflows. This changed the way I approached AI. I no longer used it just to retrieve answers. Instead, I began using it as a collaborator. Rather than asking a single question and accepting the response, I started refining my prompts, comparing outputs, and exploring multiple ways of solving a problem. AI became part of my thinking process rather than just a tool at the end of it. This shift from passive usage to active collaboration was one of the most important changes in my relationship with AI. Another noticeable difference was the variety of tools available. Initially, I only knew about one system. Over time, I became aware of multiple models and platforms, each suited for different tasks. This expansion made AI more powerful, but it also required more awareness and intentionality in how it was used. Choosing the right tool and understanding its limitations became part of the process.

Despite gaining confidence, I encountered a major setback when I attempted to build a solo Agri-Tech project using ChatGPT-4. I approached the project with the expectation that AI would guide me through most of the work. However, I was unable to complete even half of the work. This again forced me to reflect on my assumptions about AI. It can be ridiculous in giving proper answers sometimes. At the same time, I also realized that the issue was not the capability of the tool, but my own approach. I was asking vague questions and expecting precise solutions. I did not have enough technical depth to guide the AI effectively or interpret its responses. It just made me realise that prompting is not just about asking questions, it requires a clear understanding of the problem itself.

More importantly, I learned that AI cannot replace human knowledge. It can amplify what you already know, but it cannot compensate for a lack of understanding. Without a strong foundation, even accurate AI outputs can become difficult to apply. I stopped seeing it as a solution and started seeing it as a tool that requires direction. My approach improved when I later worked on another collaborative project with a friend. In that setting, I learned how to balance AI assistance with human reasoning. Instead of blindly trusting AI-generated outputs, I began reviewing and analysing them. I learned to question suggestions, test their validity, and understand the logic behind them. It actually helped me build confidence in my own understanding rather than relying entirely on AI.

My coursework deepened my perspective by revealing that AI systems are not neutral.  They often reflect hidden cultural and socioeconomic assumptions. When evaluating AI recommendations under specific budget and cultural constraints, I realized that these tools are shaped by their training data rather than objective reality. I had to prioritize context over assumed correctness. Furthermore, I learned that critical evaluation is as vital as the building process itself, as AI can generate convincing but subtly inaccurate responses that fail to fully align with complex human constraints. Throughout this process, I also reflected on the ethical aspects of using AI. There were moments when I relied on it for coursework or coding tasks, and I had to question whether I was using it as a learning tool or depending on it too much.  This has often raised an important concern: where should the line be drawn between assistance and over-reliance?

I eventually realized that while AI makes execution easier, it can dangerously reduce the mental effort required to understand concepts deeply. If used without awareness, it creates an illusion of knowledge, a sense of mastery that vanishes the moment the tool is removed.  To counter this, I shifted toward using AI more intentionally. Instead of allowing it to replace my thinking, I now use it strictly to support and refine my own original ideas.

The biggest change in my relationship with Artificial Intelligence was not about how I used the tools but about how I thought about Artificial Intelligence. I stopped seeing Artificial Intelligence as a machine that gives answers and started seeing Artificial Intelligence as a tool that finds patterns in numbers. This was a wakeup call for me. I used to be an unsure about Artificial Intelligence but now I am more careful, and I question everything it tells me. I do not look for Artificial Intelligence to give me the answer anymore. Instead, I look at what Artificial Intelligence gives me as a starting point. Then I make sure to check if it is correct.

Beyond my personal experience, I have also become more aware of how deeply AI is integrated into everyday life. It powers recommendation systems on streaming platforms, navigation tools, and various decision-making systems across industries. It has also improved efficiency and convenience, but it has also introduced challenges such as bias, privacy concerns, and the risk of over-reliance. What stands out to me is that AI is not just changing technology, it is changing how people think and interact with information.

As I look ahead to my future career in data science and AI-related fields, I see both opportunities and responsibilities. AI has the potential to enhance productivity and enable more advanced solutions, but it also requires continuous learning and critical thinking. One of my concerns is becoming too dependent on it. I want to ensure that I continue building my own understanding while using AI as a tool to enhance my capabilities.

Looking back, my journey with AI has evolved from simple and passive usage to a more thoughtful and critical engagement. I have experienced both the advantages and limitations of using AI, from building projects with limited knowledge to facing failure when relying on it too heavily. I have also found this technology to be an invaluable companion, using it for even the most random questions that pop up in my daily life, and it has benefited me in countless ways by providing instant clarity and support. These experiences have shaped how I approach AI today and I no longer see it as a replacement for human thinking. Instead, I see it as a powerful collaborator that enhances it. Its effectiveness depends entirely on how it is used. As AI continues to evolve, I believe my role is not just to use it, but to understand it, question it, and apply it responsibly.
